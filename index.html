About Me

Hello! I am a climate scientist at Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore.


With over a decade of international research experience across six countries and multicultural environments, my expertise spans ocean and climate dynamics, climate modeling, sea-level change, monsoons, extreme events, climate change impacts, and the use of big data in climate research.

I am actively involved in global scientific programs such as the CLIVAR Pacific Region Panel, UN Ocean Decade Vision 2030, WCRP Global South Inclusion Task Team, and Joint Task Team on Responsible Data Use. Through these collaborations, I aim to connect cutting-edge science with real-world solutions for resilience.


As a Senior Research Fellow at the Earth Observatory of Singapore, NTU, I address pressing climate challenges through research and teaching. My career has included leadership in international collaboration, observational field campaigns, and public engagement through education and outreach. I am committed to advancing climate science through innovative research, mentoring the next generation of scientists, and building partnerships that strengthen resilience and sustainability.
